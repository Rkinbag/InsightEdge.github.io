6. Detailed Insights and Comparison

Accenture maintains a strong position in terms of gender diversity with 47% representation, which is above the industry average of 33%. In terms of premium CV demand, Accenture stands at 10%, while competitors like IBM and Infosys offer better incentives attracting talent with 15% and 17% respectively. The hiring distribution shows that Accenture tends to prioritize IT roles at 58%, compared to competitors that focus heavily on IT at 81% (Capgemini).

In BPO and Finance domains, Accenture has a notable edge with 5% and 16% hiring focus respectively, while competitors have less emphasis here. However, several companies like Capgemini and TCS excel in hiring experienced professionals in other technical segments, while Accenture has a vast share of entry-level to mid-level roles.

For major cities, Accenture performs robustly in Bengaluru, but faces stiff competition in Hyderabad where competitors are significantly active. Overall, Accenture’s compensation packages for technical roles are competitive, though edging towards the lower side compared to some key competitors like IBM and Cognizant.

In branding context, there is a need for Accenture to highlight not only the diversity in hiring but also career progression opportunities. Enhancing employer branding through testimonials and showcasing employee achievements can foster a competitive atmosphere against rivals. Additionally, greater visibility in less saturated markets may yield potential talent pools.

In conclusion, moving forward, Accenture could benefit from enhancing its premium CV outreach and exploring talent acquisition strategies that emphasize diversity, competitive hiring packages, and branding initiatives to draw more high-quality candidates and strengthen its market position.
